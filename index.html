Citizens Police Data Project


Information about police abuse in Chicago has been hidden behind a wall of official secrecy and anonymity.

Now, after years of litigation by the Invisible Institute, police misconduct files are open to the public.

Findings

Between 2011 and 2015, 10,301 allegations of abuse were filed against 4,719 Chicago Police officers - roughly half of the 9,300 officers on the payroll.

Here is what we've found:

98.5% of police misconduct complaints resulted in no punishment for the officer

When punishments are given to officers, the vast majority lead to violation noted, reprimand, or suspentions of less than 1 week

Most officers receive only a few complaints, but a handful have accrued over 10

70% of all complaints come from African Americans

When complaints are sustained, however, they disproportionately favor whites

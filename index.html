Citizens Police Data Project

Information about police abuse in Chicago has been hidden behind a wall of official secrecy and anonymity.

Now, after years of litigation by the Invisible Institute, police misconduct files are open to the public.

Findings

Through our initial investigations, we have discovered that the Chicago Police do not analize their own complaints data. Their investigatory system is directed toward a goal of “not knowing” about the patterns of police misconduct in order to justify inaction.

Here are some of our initial findings:

Between 2011 and 2015, 40,000 allegations of abuse were filed agains Chicago Police officers.

95% resulted in no punishment
